Public, Privately

How does private ownership shape public access in New York City?

New York City's Privately Owned Public Spaces (POPS) are privately controlled sites that are legally required to provide public access. Rather than treating all POPS as equivalent, this map compares open and interior spaces while visualizing the uneven distribution of required public amenities across the city.

Reading the Map

Yellow points represent open spaces, while green points represent interior spaces. Larger points indicate a greater number of required public amenities, and darker overlaps reveal areas where multiple POPS are concentrated. Circulation-only spaces, such as through-block connections, are excluded from this study.
